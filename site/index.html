Govern the verb

Stateless admission binds each action to a signed, attenuating capability — per-action ceilings, counterparties, risk tiers, delegation. It controls what gets done, not what gets seen.

◇

Prove without revealing

A policy-satisfaction proof shows an amount stayed within bounds while the amount stays hidden — and non-compliance can't be proven at all. The verdict is checkable; the payload is not.

▣

Post-quantum & accountable

CNSA 2.0 Category-5 signatures, an append-only transparency log, and quorum receipts — so a compromised operator is caught without being trusted.

Post-quantum posture

Mapped to the AIVD/CWI/TNO PQC Migration Handbook (2nd ed., 2024). Nerion is PQC-native — built post-quantum-first, so it ships the migrated end-state. Its PS-5 tier uses the handbook's Recommended algorithms at Level 5 (CNSA 2.0); the general PS-1 tier is Category 3. Signatures use pure ML-DSA-87 (CNSA-aligned), not the handbook's more conservative hybrid-signature deployment.

Nerion is an open execution-governance protocol. The cryptographic group (ristretto255) and hash (SHAKE256) are audited primitives; the zero-knowledge protocol composition is an unaudited reference and carries no privacy guarantee until external cryptographic audit. PQC parameter levels are the design targets of the named NIST/CNSA standards, not certified results. "Govern the verb" describes an engineering design intent, not a legal opinion. No certification, audit, or non-infringement is claimed.
